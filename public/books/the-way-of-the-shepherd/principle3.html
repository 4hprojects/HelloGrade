Principle 3: Help Your Sheep Identify with You

Published on: March 13, 2025 by Henson M. Sagorsor





Help Your Sheep Identify with You: Building Trust and Connection in Leadership


Imagine this: 85% of employees say they’re more motivated to do their best work when they feel connected to their leader. That’s not just a number—it’s a game-changer. In The Way of the Shepherd, Dr. Kevin Leman and William Pentak emphasize a critical truth: leadership isn’t just about giving orders; it’s about building relationships. And that starts with helping your sheep identify with you.


What does that mean? It’s simple. Your team needs to see you as more than a boss. They need to trust you, relate to you, and feel that you genuinely care about their success. As the book puts it, “People don’t care how much you know until they know how much you care.” This principle is the foundation of authentic leadership.


So, how do you make this happen? It’s not about grand gestures or empty words. It’s about showing up consistently, being transparent, and leading by example. When your team sees you roll up your sleeves and work alongside them, they’ll start to see you as one of their own.


In this article, we’ll explore actionable strategies to help your sheep identify with you, from fostering open communication to demonstrating vulnerability and empathy. Whether you’re leading a small team or an entire organization, these insights will help you build trust, inspire loyalty, and create a culture of connection.


Ready to transform your leadership approach? Let’s dive in. Because when your team identifies with you, they don’t just follow—they thrive.

How to Help Your Sheep Identify with You


While Principle 2 is about understanding your team, Principle 3 is about ensuring they understand and connect with you. Here’s how to build that mutual bond:


1. Be Transparent About Your Vision

Share your goals, values, and vision for the team. When your team understands where you’re leading them and why, they’ll feel more aligned with your direction.


2. Lead with Authenticity

Be genuine in your actions and decisions. Your team can sense when you’re being authentic, and this builds trust and respect.


3. Show Vulnerability

Don’t be afraid to share your challenges and mistakes. Vulnerability humanizes you and makes you more relatable to your team.


4. Consistently Demonstrate Your Values

Align your actions with your words. If you value teamwork, collaborate openly. If you value innovation, encourage creative thinking. Your team will mirror what they see in you.


5. Celebrate Shared Successes

When the team achieves a milestone, celebrate it together. Highlight how their efforts contributed to the success, reinforcing their connection to you and the team’s mission.


Lead with Connection, Inspire with Trust


Leadership isn’t about authority—it’s about connection. When your team identifies with you, they don’t just follow your direction; they believe in your vision and trust your leadership. By being transparent, authentic, and consistent, you create a bond that transforms your team into a united, motivated force.


Remember, the principles from The Way of the Shepherd aren’t just theories—they’re actionable steps to becoming the leader your team needs. Start today by helping your sheep identify with you. Build trust, share your story, and lead by example. The results will speak for themselves.
