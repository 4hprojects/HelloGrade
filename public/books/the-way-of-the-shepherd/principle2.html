Principle 2: Discover the Shape of Your Sheep

Published on: March 6, 2025 by Henson M. Sagorsor




Discover the Shape of Your Sheep: Unlocking the Art of Leadership


What if I told you that 70% of employees feel disengaged at work? It’s a staggering number, and it’s not just a statistic—it’s a wake-up call. In The Way of the Shepherd, Dr. Kevin Leman and William Pentak reveal a timeless truth: great leadership isn’t about managing tasks; it’s about understanding people. And that starts with discovering the shape of your sheep.


Yes, your sheep. Think of your team as a flock—each member unique, with their own strengths, quirks, and needs. The best leaders don’t herd blindly; they take the time to truly know their people. As the book puts it, “You can’t lead sheep you don’t know.” It’s a simple yet profound idea that transforms how we approach leadership.


So, how do you uncover the shape of your sheep? It starts with intentional observation, meaningful conversations, and a willingness to adapt your leadership style. This isn’t about micromanaging or coddling—it’s about empowering your team to thrive by meeting them where they are.


In this article, we’ll dive into actionable strategies to help you discover the shape of your sheep, from identifying individual strengths to creating an environment where everyone feels valued. Whether you’re a seasoned leader or just stepping into a management role, these insights will help you build a stronger, more engaged team.


Ready to transform your leadership approach? Let’s get started. Because when you know the shape of your sheep, you don’t just lead—you inspire.


How to Discover the Shape of Your Sheep


Discovering the shape of your sheep is about understanding your team members as individuals. Here are actionable strategies to help you get started:


1. Observe and Listen

Great leaders are keen observers. Pay attention to how your team members work, communicate, and interact with others. Listen actively during conversations—what excites them? What challenges do they face? This will help you identify their unique strengths and motivations.


2. Have Meaningful One-on-Ones

Regular one-on-one meetings are a powerful way to build trust and understand your team members better. Ask open-ended questions like, “What do you enjoy most about your work?” or “Where do you feel you can grow?” These conversations reveal insights into their aspirations and concerns.


3. Adapt Your Leadership Style

Not everyone responds to the same leadership approach. Some team members thrive with autonomy, while others need more guidance. Tailor your leadership style to meet the needs of each individual, ensuring they feel supported and valued.


4. Create a Safe Environment

Your team should feel safe to express their ideas, ask questions, and make mistakes. Foster a culture of openness and trust, where everyone feels comfortable being themselves. This encourages authenticity and innovation.


5. Celebrate Individuality

Recognize and celebrate the unique contributions of each team member. Whether it’s a shout-out in a team meeting or a personalized note of appreciation, acknowledging their efforts boosts morale and reinforces their value to the team.

Key Takeaways


  • Know Your Sheep: Leadership starts with understanding your team members as individuals.
  • Lead with Intentionality: Be deliberate in your actions and decisions, always considering the impact on your team.
  • Empower Your Flock: Provide the tools, support, and environment your team needs to succeed.
  • Build Trust: Trust is the foundation of strong leadership. Be consistent, transparent, and reliable.
  • Inspire Growth: Encourage your team to grow both personally and professionally, and lead by example.
