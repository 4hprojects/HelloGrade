Module 1: The Fundamentals of Prompting

Every AI system, from Google Gemini to ChatGPT, relies on well-structured prompts to generate meaningful responses. The more specific your instructions, the better the AI output.

Google’s Prompt Engineering Essentials introduces a five-step framework to help users craft effective prompts. This method is essential for anyone using AI automation, AI-powered content creation, or data analysis tools.

The Five-Step Prompt Framework

Google recommends structuring prompts using these five essential elements:

  1. Task: Clearly define what you want the AI to do.
  2. Context: Provide details that help the AI understand the request.
  3. References: Add examples or additional information to refine the output.
  4. Evaluate: Assess the AI’s response to check for relevance and accuracy.
  5. Iterate: Modify your prompt and refine it to improve the result.

Example: AI-Powered Gift Recommendations

Let's apply this framework to a real-world scenario. Imagine your friend loves anime, and you want AI to suggest a unique birthday gift. A simple prompt might be:

"Suggest a gift related to anime for my friend's birthday."

While this works, we can improve it by adding a persona and format:

"Act as an anime merchandise expert and suggest a birthday gift for my friend, who loves Solo Leveling and Naruto. Organize the results into a table, listing gift ideas, price ranges, and where to buy them."

By specifying persona, preferences, and format, we refine the AI’s response to provide detailed and relevant recommendations.

Always Be Iterating (ABI)

AI prompting isn’t one-and-done. It’s an iterative process. Google calls this approach "Always Be Iterating" (ABI)—refining your prompts until the AI delivers the best possible output.

Module 2: Designing Prompts for Everyday Tasks

AI is revolutionizing productivity. Whether you're drafting emails, summarizing reports, or brainstorming content ideas, effective prompts can cut your work time in half. In this module, Google provides real-world examples to optimize your day-to-day AI-powered workflows.

AI-Powered Email Writing

One of the most common AI use cases is automating email drafts. Here’s how a simple prompt can be improved using Google’s prompt engineering framework:

❌ Basic Prompt: "Write an email about our new gym schedule." ✅ Optimized Prompt: "I’m a gym manager. Write a concise, professional, and friendly email announcing a new schedule change to my staff. Highlight that the Monday/Wednesday/Friday Cardio Blast class has moved from 7:00 AM to 6:00 AM. Keep the email easy to skim."

Why This Works:
- Role Assignment: "I’m a gym manager" helps AI tailor the email’s tone.
- Details & Focus: Specific time changes help prevent confusion.
- Formatting Request: Asking for a concise and skimmable email improves readability.

Using AI for Brainstorming & Content Generation

Struggling to come up with ideas? AI can help with brainstorming blog topics, social media posts, or business strategies. Example prompt for content creation:

"Act as a digital marketing strategist. Generate 10 engaging blog post ideas for a company that specializes in remote work software. Include SEO-friendly titles and focus on topics that improve productivity, remote team collaboration, and workflow automation."

Why This Works:
- Persona Definition: "Act as a digital marketing strategist" ensures expert-level insights.
- Specific Requirements: Asking for SEO-friendly titles improves searchability and engagement.
- Focused Topics: AI generates industry-relevant content, not random ideas.

Automating Summaries & Report Generation

AI can also summarize complex documents into clear, digestible formats. Example prompt for report summarization:

"Summarize this 5-page quarterly sales report into a 1-paragraph executive summary. Focus on key growth metrics, revenue trends, and future projections."

Why This Works:
- Concise Output: Specifies length and key focus areas.
- Data-driven Request: AI prioritizes growth metrics and revenue trends.
- Tailored to Business Leaders: Ideal for executives and decision-makers.

Building Your AI Prompt Library

To maximize efficiency, save your most effective AI prompts in a personalized prompt library. Over time, you can refine them for even better results. Here’s a quick reference:

  • 📧 Email Automation: "Write a formal, concise email about [topic]..."
  • 📝 Content Generation: "Generate 10 blog ideas about [industry]..."
  • 📊 Data Insights: "Analyze trends in this dataset and provide insights..."
  • 📑 Report Summarization: "Summarize this document into key points..."
  • 📢 Social Media Posts: "Create an engaging Twitter thread about [topic]..."

By organizing your prompts, you save time, maintain consistency, and refine AI-generated responses. Next, we’ll explore how AI can analyze data and build presentations with minimal effort.

Module 3: Using AI for Data Analysis & Presentations

AI isn’t just for text-based tasks. With the right prompts, AI can analyze spreadsheets, uncover trends, and even automate presentations. This is especially useful for professionals working in finance, research, business strategy, and marketing.

AI-Powered Spreadsheet Analysis

Instead of manually sifting through Google Sheets or Excel, AI can help you identify patterns, generate insights, and suggest next steps.

Example prompt for analyzing sales data:

"I have a Google Sheet with store sales data, including daily customer counts, item categories, and revenue. Analyze the data and provide insights on trends, seasonal changes, and customer preferences."

Why This Works:
- Data-driven Query: AI prioritizes patterns in customer behavior.
- Actionable Insights: The AI provides next-step recommendations for increasing sales.
- Automates Manual Work: No need to scroll through thousands of rows in a spreadsheet.

Identifying Trends & Generating Insights

AI can highlight key performance indicators (KPIs), predict future trends, and compare datasets for deeper insights. Example prompt for trend detection:

"Analyze this e-commerce dataset and identify the top three buying trends over the last six months. Provide recommendations on how we can capitalize on these trends."

Why This Works:
- Extracts Market Insights: AI spots trends faster than manual analysis.
- Provides Business Strategies: AI doesn’t just summarize—it recommends actions.
- Helps Decision-Making: AI-powered insights improve business growth strategies.

AI-Powered Presentation Creation

AI can structure slides, generate bullet points, and ensure presentations stay concise and engaging. Instead of manually designing slides, let AI do the heavy lifting.

Example prompt for slide deck generation:

"Create a 10-slide investor pitch deck for a tech startup focused on AI automation. The slides should include: company overview, market opportunity, competitive landscape, product features, revenue model, and investment ask."

Why This Works:
- Saves Time: No need to manually outline slides.
- Ensures Structured Flow: AI organizes the presentation logically.
- Customizable Output: AI allows adjustments to tone, focus, and length.

Automating Data Reports & Insights

AI can also transform raw data into professional reports with structured summaries, tables, and visual insights. Example prompt for automated reports:

"Summarize this monthly sales report into a 1-page executive briefing. Include key performance metrics, revenue breakdowns, and a forecast for the next quarter."

Why This Works:
- Turns Data into Meaningful Reports: AI summarizes complex spreadsheets into clear takeaways.
- Saves Hours of Work: AI automates what would normally take hours of manual effort.
- Improves Business Decision-Making: AI-generated reports help leaders act faster.

Module 4: AI as a Creative or Expert Partner

AI can do more than automate tasks—it can think, reason, and assist in complex problem-solving. In this module, Google introduces advanced prompting techniques that enhance AI’s critical thinking abilities.

Prompt Chaining: Building Step-by-Step AI Workflows

Prompt chaining is a method where multiple prompts are connected in a logical sequence, allowing AI to build on previous responses. This technique is useful for:

  • Developing structured content creation workflows.
  • Guiding AI through multi-step research tasks.
  • Creating automated customer support chatbots.

Example prompt for writing an article using prompt chaining:

Step 1: "Summarize the latest AI trends from this research report into three key insights." Step 2: "Expand on each insight with real-world examples and supporting statistics." Step 3: "Format the final output into a 1000-word blog post with headings and bullet points."

Why This Works:
- Ensures Logical Flow: AI builds on previous responses instead of generating random content.
- Breaks Down Complex Tasks: Each step focuses on a specific aspect of the request.
- Maintains Content Quality: Structured steps reduce AI hallucinations.

Chain of Thought Prompting: Improving AI’s Reasoning

Chain of Thought (CoT) prompting encourages AI to explain its reasoning step by step. This technique is especially valuable for problem-solving and logical reasoning.

Example prompt for improving decision-making:

"A business is deciding between expanding into e-commerce or opening a physical store. Analyze the pros and cons of both options and explain your reasoning step by step."

Why This Works:
- Forces AI to Justify Its Answers: AI can’t just output generic advice—it must provide logical reasoning.
- Improves Transparency: Users can evaluate and refine AI's thought process.
- Better for Critical Thinking: Ideal for business strategy, math, and legal analysis.

Tree of Thought Prompting: Exploring Multiple Paths

Tree of Thought (ToT) prompting takes Chain of Thought further, allowing AI to explore multiple reasoning paths simultaneously. This is useful for creative problem-solving, brainstorming, and decision-making.

Example prompt for brainstorming business ideas:

"Imagine three startup founders are brainstorming a new AI-powered productivity app. Each founder has a unique perspective: one focuses on user experience, one on automation, and one on scalability. Outline their three ideas and evaluate the pros and cons of each."

Why This Works:
- Encourages Diverse Thinking: AI generates multiple solutions rather than just one.
- Useful for Business & Research: Great for developing strategies and evaluating alternatives.
- Enhances AI’s Creative Output: AI thinks through different perspectives rather than giving one standard answer.

Building AI Agents: Personalized AI Assistants

AI agents act as specialized virtual assistants that can handle specific tasks like coaching, content review, or expert advice.

Example prompt for creating a startup advisor AI agent:

"Act as a startup advisor with expertise in SaaS business models. My startup is building a B2B AI automation tool. Help me refine my go-to-market strategy, focusing on customer acquisition, pricing models, and product positioning."

Why This Works:
- Defines AI’s Role Clearly: The AI behaves as a subject-matter expert.
- Provides Focused Insights: AI stays within a specific domain rather than being too general.
- Enhances AI-Powered Decision-Making: Helps founders, marketers, and professionals.

Combining Advanced Techniques for Maximum AI Potential

The most powerful results come from combining these techniques:

  • 📈 Use Prompt Chaining to break down complex tasks into step-by-step instructions.
  • 🤔 Apply Chain of Thought to force AI to explain its reasoning.
  • 🌳 Leverage Tree of Thought for exploring multiple solutions before making a decision.
  • 🤖 Build AI Agents to handle specific expert tasks for ongoing assistance.

AI isn’t just a tool—it’s an intelligent collaborator. Mastering these techniques will take your AI capabilities to the next level.
